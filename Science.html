Indian satellites

Satellite

A satellite is anything that orbits a planet or a star.

There are two types of Satellite-
Natural satellites
- A natural satellite is a celestial body that orbits a planet, dwarf planet, or asteroid. Unlike artificial satellites, which are human-made, natural satellites are formed through natural processes such as gravitational capture, accretion, or co-formation with their parent planet.
Artificial satellites
- are those satellites that humans have launched into orbit around the earth.

Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO)

Indian Space Research Organization (ISRO) is the space agency of India. The space activities in India started with the setting up of Indian National Committee for Space Research [INCOSPAR] in 1962. ISRO was established in August1969. Vikram Sarabhai, the founding father of Indian space program.ISRO] established in 1969 took the space program to all together new level in the coming years with programs characterized by a vision to use space technology for national and societal development.
The Aryabhata spacecraft, named after the famous Indian astronomer, was India's first satellite; it was completely designed and fabricated in India and launched on April 19, 1975.
Over a relatively short span of time, India has emerged as a prominent global player in space exploration through successful missions such as Chandrayaan series, Manga lyaan etc.

GSAT Series

GSAT series - These satellites are primarily dedicated to telecommunications services such as television broadcasting, DTH television and telecommunication.

NavIC

NavIC [Navigation with Indian Constellation] - This regional navigation satellite system provides positioning, navigation and timing services for India. It supports a variety of applications including transportation, resource monitoring, surveying location- based services etc.

Benifits of Satellite

India's space capabilities has also enabled the country to provide a range of services including-
• -Weather forecast- supported by the INSAT series of satellites, which operate from geostationary orbit.
• -Satellite-based search and rescue-supported by satellites in low Earth orbit.Example-INSAT-3A satellite support a Satellite Aided Research and Rescue system.
• -Climate studies- help monitor environmental changes and study climate patterns. NISAR {NASA ISRO Synthetic Aperture Radar} satellite helps to study climate change.
• Agriculture-space data supports precision farming and crop monitoring. Examples- INSAT-3D and INSAT-3DR.
• -Education and Healthcare- telemedicine and tele-education services for remote areas. Example: GSAT-3 also known as EDUSAT was the first dedicated educational satellite.

Conclusion

Over the past few decades, the world has been made aware of India's space exploration prowess through successful missions which has made India the fifth amongst spacefaring nations having end to end capabilities in space research and development.
